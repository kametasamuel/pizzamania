About Pizza

Pizza is a beloved and iconic dish enjoyed by people all over the world. Originating from Italy, it has become a global culinary sensation. The basic concept of pizza involves a flatbread crust topped with a variety of ingredients such as tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings, which can include vegetables, meats, and herbs.

One of the most popular styles of pizza is the Neapolitan pizza, characterized by its thin, chewy crust and simple, fresh toppings. Another well-known style is the New York-style pizza, which features a large, foldable slice with a crispy yet chewy crust.

Over the years, pizza has evolved and adapted to different regional tastes, giving rise to various regional styles such as Chicago deep-dish pizza, Sicilian pizza, and California-style pizza. Each style has its unique characteristics, reflecting the culinary traditions and preferences of the specific region.

In addition to traditional styles, the pizza industry has also witnessed a surge in creative and innovative variations. Gourmet pizzas now abound, featuring unique flavor combinations and exotic toppings like arugula, prosciutto, truffle oil, and even fruit.

Pizza has become a versatile food that caters to diverse dietary preferences and restrictions. Vegetarian and vegan options have gained popularity, offering plant-based alternatives for cheese and meat toppings. Gluten-free crusts have also become widely available, allowing those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease to enjoy this beloved dish.

Pizza Types

Neapolitan Pizza

Neapolitan is the original pizza. This delicious pie dates all the way back to 18th century in Naples, Italy. During this time, the poorer citizens of this seaside city frequently purchased food that was cheap and could be eaten quickly.

Chicago Pizza

Chicago pizza, also commonly referred to as deep-dish pizza, gets its name from the city it was invented in. During the early 1900’s, Italian immigrants in the windy city were searching for something similar to the Neapolitan pizza that they knew and loved.

New York-Style Pizza

With its characteristic large, foldable slices and crispy outer crust, New York-style pizza is one of America’s most famous regional pizza types. Originally a variation of Neapolitan-style pizza, the New York slice has taken on a fame all its own, with some saying its unique flavor has to do with the minerals present in New York’s tap water supply.

Sicilian Pizza

Sicilian pizza, also known as "sfincione," provides a thick cut of pizza with pillowy dough, a crunchy crust, and robust tomato sauce. This square-cut pizza is served with or without cheese, and often with the cheese underneath the sauce to prevent the pie from becoming soggy.
